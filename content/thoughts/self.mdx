# Self

<div className="text-center mb-12 text-sm uppercase tracking-widest opacity-50">
  Reflections • February 2026
</div>

reasoning through everything and seeing your dos as musts rather than products of willpower and motives. we can't ask the world to be fair or to always follow intellectual standards that make it better over time. but we can ask that of ourselves—the ask is near zero when it's directed inward, I believe.

we think things need some kind of beautiful north star to make them worth the time and effort. but in most circumstances you don't 100 percent know how deep the roots need to grow, and the deeper they get, the stronger the tree becomes.

you have to ask yourself questions:

> Socrates: Know Thyself

and find answers to those questions continuously. I doubt there's a stop to that. I like to see it as a lifetime thing. but are they the right questions to be asking yourself? that's subjective, but you can only find out by asking. seeing the self as the material to be studied by the self. you have to be really honest with yourself. you should never lie to yourself:

> **Fyodor Dostoevsky**  
> The man who lies to himself... comes to a point where he cannot distinguish the truth within him.

seeking improvements from your dos every time and reevaluating. the self can be powerful, yet it can be its own worst enemy if it don't keep in check. it has to stay patient in the process and reevaluate itself.
